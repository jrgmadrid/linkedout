# LinkedOut

Chrome extension (Manifest V3) that hides promoted posts, social-graph noise,
and reposts from the LinkedIn feed. Filtered posts collapse into a native-
looking "Hidden post" card showing the reason, with a per-post **Show**
button to reveal (the card stays as a slim "Post shown" bar with a **Hide**
button, so it's reversible). Click the toolbar icon to pick filters:

- **Promoted posts** (on by default)
- **Reaction spillover** вЂ” "Jane Doe likes this"
- **Comment spillover** вЂ” "Jane Doe commented on this"
- **Reposts** вЂ” "Jane Doe reposted this"
- **Follow spillover** вЂ” "Jane Doe follows Acme Corp"
- **Suggested posts** вЂ” "Recommended for you"
- **News & games** вЂ” the "LinkedIn News" / "Today's puzzles" rail widgets
- **Slop badge** (on by default) вЂ” engagement-bait posts stay visible but get
  a "Likely slop" pill; see below
- **Chip hide filters** (off by default) вЂ” each chip (Broetry, Engagement
  bait, Ad spam, Undisclosed promo, Certified slop) can also collapse its
  posts behind a placeholder; judge-minted chips collapse when the verdict
  lands

## How it works

Detection is calibrated against LinkedIn's 2026 frontend (hashed CSS-module
classes, `componentkey` attributes), harvested from a live feed вЂ” see
`harvest.js`, a debug exporter kept around for re-tuning when LinkedIn shifts.

- Post root: `div[role="listitem"][componentkey*="FeedType"]`, with the
  classic-DOM selectors kept as fallback (LinkedIn A/B tests rendering stacks).
- Promoted posts: `[aria-label="View Sponsored Content"]` (an accessibility
  disclosure) or a standalone "Promoted" label leaf, exact-matched against
  several locale strings.
- Socially-surfaced posts: the surfacing header ("Jane Doe commented") is
  always the first text in the post, before the author block вЂ” patterns are
  matched only against that head segment, so body text can't false-positive.

A content script watches the feed with a `MutationObserver` (LinkedIn is an
SPA, so posts stream in continuously) and classifies each post exactly once
into a `data-dp-reasons` attribute. The set of enabled filters is written to a
`data-dp-hide` attribute on `<html>`, and CSS rules generated by `content.js` from `DP_FILTERS` intersect the two вЂ”
so toggling a filter in the popup applies instantly, in both directions,
without re-scanning anything. Settings sync via `chrome.storage.sync`.

## Slop badge

Posts are scored for *slop* вЂ” formulaic engagement-farming prose. It's an
aesthetic judgment about the text, deliberately **not** an "AI-generated"
claim: a human-written hustle post earns its chips just fine. Signals group
into three families, each rendering its own chip when it fires: **Broetry**
(line-stacking, "It's not X, it's Y", em-dashes, buzzwords), **Engagement
bait** ("Agree?", repost CTAs, hashtag walls, reach hacks like "link in the
first comment", рќ—ірќ—®рќ—ёрќ—І-рќ—Їрќ—јрќ—№рќ—± Unicode styling), and **Ad spam** (ALL-CAPS
shouting, link piles, contact blocks). Pseudo-bold text is also folded back
to ASCII before scoring, so it can't smuggle buzzwords past the lexicons. A judge-confirmed post gains a filled **Certified slop** chip.
Hover any chip for its itemized offense receipt.

A fourth chip, **Undisclosed promo**, is minted only by the judge: promotion
disguised as personal content or free value. Regex never convicts on intent вЂ”
cheap promo markers ("register now", discount codes, launch phrasing) merely
send a suspected post to the judge, who lets honest promotion walk: job
posts, launch announcements, and event invites that say what they are keep
their dignity. The verdict is independent of the slop score, so a
well-written stealth ad wears the chip anyway.

Two tiers, two brains:

1. **Heuristic scorer** (`slop.js`) вЂ” free, local, synchronous; runs on every
   post's commentary. Each family chips independently at its own points
   threshold; clearly fine posts are done.
2. **LLM judge** (`worker.js`) вЂ” posts whose aggregate score lands in the
   ambiguous middle band (or that trip a promo marker below it) are sent to
   an OpenAI-compatible endpoint (DeepSeek by default) from the background
   service worker. The judge can certify
   (filled chip) or clear the heuristic chips outright вЂ” the mercy path for
   sincere posts that merely look like slop. Verdicts are cached per post,
   judged at most once, and every failure mode falls back to the heuristic
   chips вЂ” the feed never waits on the network.

The judge is optional. To enable it, copy `config.local.example.js` to
`config.local.js` (gitignored) and fill in your API key; without it the
heuristic tier runs alone and the worker answers every request with a shrug.
Scoring weights are calibrated against a labeled post set вЂ” run
`node test/slop.test.js`.

## Caveats

- Purely a visual filter вЂ” LinkedIn still counts impressions server-side.
- Header pattern-matching is English-only (promoted-label detection is not).
  Using LinkedIn in another language means the social filters won't match
  until their patterns are added to `DP_FILTERS` in `filters.js`. The slop
  scorer's signal lexicons are English-only too.
- "Reposts" covers both header-style reposts ("вЂ¦ reposted this") and bare
  reposts the new UI renders as an embedded original with no header (detected
  by a second "Visibility:" disclosure plus back-to-back timestamps with no
  commentary between). A repost where someone added their own commentary is
  left alone вЂ” that's usually the desirable behavior.
- Everything is additive, so if LinkedIn shifts the DOM again the worst case
  is a filter silently stops matching вЂ” nothing breaks. Re-run the popup's
  "Export feed markers" to harvest fresh signals and re-tune.
